# Hooks Library — Scroll-Stoppers for DGD Education

50+ opening lines, all compliance-safe (curiosity, not profit). Swap in your topic. The hook is ~80% of performance — write 5, pick 1.

> Every hook here teaches an idea. None promises money. Keep it that way. → [do & don't](../compliance/do-and-dont-language.md)

## The 3 highest-performing 2026 frameworks

### Contrarian Claim ("everything you think is wrong")
- "Most 'crypto payments' aren't actually money. Let me prove it."
- "Saving money is making you poorer. Here's the math."
- "Bitcoin isn't really used as money — and that's by design."
- "Your bank account is a slow leak you can't see."

### Mistake Warning ("you're doing X wrong")
- "If you think inflation is just 'prices going up,' you're missing the real story."
- "Everyone gets 'sound money' wrong. Here's what it actually means."
- "Stop thinking about crypto as a lottery ticket. Think about this instead."

### List Tease ("there are N things…")
- "There are 6 things real money needs. Most currencies fail at least one."
- "4 numbers decide what a cryptocurrency is actually worth."
- "3 economists figured out money 100 years ago. We forgot."

## By curiosity trigger

### Shocking number
- "The US dollar lost over 96% of its value since 1913."
- "The money supply grew 7,000% in one century."
- "A 2% inflation target cuts your savings in half every 35 years."

### The question hook
- "What makes something *money* and not just stuff?"
- "Who actually decides what a coin is worth?"
- "What if money had rules no government could rewrite?"

### The "test/secret" hook
- "Here's a 5-second test for whether something is really money."
- "There's a reason every altcoin moves with Bitcoin. Nobody explains it."

### The story/history hook
- "In the 1920s the stock market had no way to value anything. Sound familiar?"
- "Gold became money for 5 reasons. Code can now copy all 5."
- "Three thinkers — Menger, Mises, Hayek — predicted all of this."

### The analogy hook
- "Imagine a ruler that changed length every day. That's the dollar."
- "The meter never changes. Why should the measure of value?"

### The myth-bust hook
- "'Crypto is just gambling' — partly true, and here's the part that isn't."
- "No, more coins doesn't mean richer. Here's why."

## Positioning hooks: "safe harbor" & anti-degen
(See [positioning & audiences](positioning-and-audiences.md) and [safe-harbor page](../dgd/positioning-safe-harbor.md).)

### "Boring on purpose" / anti-degen
- "This might be the most boring coin in crypto. On purpose."
- "Crypto Twitter is 99% degen plays. Here's the 1% nobody posts."
- "Tired of getting rekt? There's a different idea of what money is."
- "Everyone's chasing the next 100x. This was built so there's nothing to chase."
- "The most contrarian thing in crypto right now: boring, sound money."

### "Safe harbor" (legal, not financial)
- "'Safe harbor' in crypto does NOT mean what you think."
- "Is your coin a security? There's finally a test. Let's run it."
- "One phrase in the SEC's new definition decides everything: 'rather than from expectations of profit.'"
- "Why would a coin *want* to be called boring infrastructure?"

> ⚠️ These hook a speculative audience but the payload must stay educational. Never tip into "ape in," "100x," or "safe investment." → [do & don't](../compliance/do-and-dont-language.md)

## Hook engineering checklist
- **≤ 8 words** if possible; punchy.
- Creates an **open loop** you must close.
- Pairs with a **strong first frame** (the visual is half the hook). → [images & thumbnails](../tools/images-and-thumbnails.md)
- **No banned words** (invest, moon, returns…). → [do & don't](../compliance/do-and-dont-language.md)
- Spoken **and** on-screen as text.

## Pro move: the "loop-back" ending
End by reopening curiosity to drive replays/follows:
- "…and that's only the first of the six. Follow for the rest."
- "Watch it again — the number in second 2 changes how you read the rest."
