# What Actually Makes Short-Form Spread (2026)

The mechanics of reach — applied to **educational** DGD content. None of this requires hype; the best explainers go viral on curiosity, not promises.

## The 1-second truth
The old "first 3 seconds" rule was built for 2018–2022 long-form discovery. On a swipeable short-form feed in 2026, the keep-or-scroll decision happens **at or before the 1-second mark**. Your **first frame + first words** are the whole ballgame. Over 70% of viral shorts had a strong hook in the first seconds, and videos with strong hooks see **3–5× higher completion**. → [hooks library](hooks-library.md)

## What the algorithm rewards (Shorts, 2026)
Modern short-form ranking weighs, roughly in order:
1. **Replay rate** — did they watch it again?
2. **Watch-through / completion** — did they finish?
3. **Click-through / "stop scroll"** — did the first frame stop them?
4. **Shares** — did they send it to someone?
5. **Like-to-view ratio** and **comments**.

Notably, for Shorts, **raw watch time matters less than replays and shares.** That favors **short, dense, re-watchable** explainers — perfect for one-idea money concepts.

## The five levers you control

### 1. Hook (stop the scroll)
One second to create an **information gap** or **curiosity loop**. The three most reliably viral 2026 hook types: **Contrarian Claim**, **Mistake Warning**, **List Tease**. → [hooks](hooks-library.md)

### 2. Retention (keep them)
- One idea per video. Cut everything else.
- Visual change every ~2 seconds.
- "Open loop" early, close it at the end ("…but here's the part nobody mentions →").
- Captions always (mute viewing).

### 3. Payoff (reward them)
Deliver the promised insight clearly. A satisfied viewer replays, shares, and follows. Educational content has a built-in advantage: **people share things that make them look smart.**

### 4. Shareability (multiply them)
Make it *"send this to someone"* content: a surprising fact about inflation, a clean mental model, a myth busted. "Did you know the dollar lost 96% of its value since 1913?" is eminently shareable.

### 5. Consistency (compound them)
Post regularly (series help). The algorithm and your audience both reward a recognizable, repeated format. → [content calendar](../templates/content-calendar-and-series.md)

## Educational virality without hype (your lane)
You don't need "🚀 to the moon" energy — that's banned anyway ([discipline](../compliance/communications-discipline.md)). Your viral engine is:
- **Curiosity** ("Is this even money?")
- **Counter-intuition** ("Most crypto payments aren't actually money. Here's the test.")
- **Usefulness** ("The 6 things real money needs")
- **Clarity** (make a hard idea feel easy)

Science/finance explainer creators prove this works at scale without selling anything.

## Test & iterate
- Check the **retention graph** for the exact drop-off second; fix that beat next time.
- A/B the **hook and thumbnail** — same video, two openings.
- Track **CTR, replay, share** more than raw views.
- Double down on whichever **topic** and **format** overperforms.
