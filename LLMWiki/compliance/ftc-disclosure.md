# FTC Disclosure — When and How to Say "Sponsored"

If you receive **anything of value** for posting — DGD recognition, free products, perks — you have a **material connection** and US FTC rules require you to disclose it clearly. This is separate from, and in addition to, the Foundation's [communications discipline](communications-discipline.md).

## When disclosure is required

You must disclose if you get **any** of: DGD recognition through the [promotional pathway](../dgd/participation-pathways.md), payment, free products, event invites, discounts, or "any other form of value exchange." The FTC treats a missing disclosure on a financial/crypto promotion as **high-risk** because of potential financial harm.

When in doubt: disclose. There's no penalty for over-disclosing.

## How to disclose correctly (2026 standard)

The FTC's bar in 2026 is that disclosures must be **obvious to an average viewer immediately** — unavoidable, before they engage, not buried.

**Do all three for short-form video:**

1. **Spoken** — say it out loud in the **first few seconds**: *"This is a sponsored educational video — I take part in Digital Gold's ambassador program."*
2. **On-screen text** — a clear overlay (high-contrast, readable) in the opening, e.g. "Sponsored / Ambassador content."
3. **Caption** — put **#Ad** or **#Sponsored** at the **very start** of the caption, not after a wall of hashtags.

**Use the platform's built-in tool too:**
- Instagram/Facebook: turn on the **Paid Partnership** label. → [platform-policies.md#instagram-reels](platform-policies.md#instagram-reels)
- TikTok: toggle the **branded content / paid partnership** disclosure.
- YouTube: tick **"contains paid promotion."**
- X: use the **paid partnership** label.

## What does NOT count (FTC will reject these)

- ❌ "#ad" at the end of 15 hashtags.
- ❌ Tiny, low-contrast text that flashes by.
- ❌ "Thanks to my partners" with no clear sponsorship language.
- ❌ Disclosure only in a pinned comment.

## Why this is doubly important for DGD

A crypto/financial promotion with a missing disclosure is exactly what the FTC has been enforcing against (celebrity crypto settlements in 2024–2025). Penalties can run **$51,000+ per violation** and **stack across every post**. And remember: a disclosure says you were *rewarded* — it does **not** let you make investment claims. You still cannot promise returns or predict price. Disclosure + discipline, always both.

## Plain-English disclosure scripts (copy these)

- Opening line: *"Quick heads-up: I'm part of the Digital Gold ambassador program, so this is sponsored — and it's educational, not financial advice."*
- On-screen (0–3s): **"SPONSORED · AMBASSADOR · NOT FINANCIAL ADVICE"**
- Caption start: **"#Ad — educational, not financial advice. ..."**
