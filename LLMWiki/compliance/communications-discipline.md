# The Golden Rules of DGD Communication

**The single most important page in this wiki.** These rules come directly from the Foundation's own commitments in the White Paper. They are not optional.

## Where these rules come from

White Paper §12.11, "Marketing and Communications Discipline," states the Foundation's communications:

> *"…do not characterize DGD as an investment, do not project specific price appreciation, do not promise returns from any Foundation activity, and do not solicit participation on the basis of expected profits."*

It continues: communications **describe** DGD's design, the CFV framework, the monetary-reform mission, and the mechanics of validation — so that participants decide "based on their own evaluation of the design and the framework, not on the basis of Foundation representations about profit potential" (WP §12.11).

This discipline explicitly applies to the Foundation's **authorized communications channels** — and as a rewarded ambassador you are speaking into that same public record.

## The Five Golden Rules

### 1. Never call it an investment
DGD is **money / a monetary system / a currency design** — not an investment, asset to flip, or opportunity. The White Paper says plainly: *"This is not a speculative instrument"* (WP Abstract).

### 2. Never predict the price
No "it'll hit $X," no "going to $100k," no "early," no charts implying future gains. The price curve is a **distribution mechanism**, not a forecast (WP §5). You may explain *how the curve works*; you may not say *where it's going for your benefit*.

### 3. Never promise returns or profit
No "returns," "ROI," "passive income," "you'll make money," "I made money." No party earns income from DGD's operation by design — there are no staking rewards and fees are burned (WP Abstract, §12).

### 4. Never solicit on the basis of profit
Don't say "buy now," "get in before…," "don't miss out." You can invite people to **learn**, **read the white paper**, and **evaluate the design** — not to chase gains.

### 5. Always stay educational and honest
Describe design, economics, history, and mechanics. Acknowledge uncertainty openly — the White Paper itself does: it calls its non-security position *"a reasoned legal analysis, not a guarantee"* and admits a regulator could reach a different conclusion (WP §12.16). Honesty is on-brand.

## What you CAN do (lots)

- Explain inflation, sound money, and Austrian economics.
- Teach the Six Pillars, the CFV/DGSB framework, PoP distribution, single-price circulation.
- Give accurate, sourced "how it's designed to work" breakdowns.
- Share your honest interest in the **ideas**.
- Point people to the white paper and official channels to judge for themselves.

## The mindset

You're a **teacher and explainer**, like a good science communicator — not a salesperson and not a hype account. The best DGD content would be valuable to someone who never acquires a single coin. If your video only makes sense as a reason to buy, rewrite it.

## If someone asks you "will I make money?"
Safe answer: *"I can't tell you that, and I won't — I'm not a financial advisor and this isn't investment advice. What I can do is explain how the system is designed. Whether it's right for you is your call after your own research."*
