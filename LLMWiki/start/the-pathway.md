# The complete pathway: idea → posted video

This is the single, ordered path. Follow it top to bottom and you never have to wonder
"what now?" Each step says **what you're doing**, the **one resource to open**, and what
**"done" looks like**. We carry one running example the whole way — an episode called
*"Why your money covers a little less each year"* — see its finished pieces in
[a worked example](worked-example.md), and keep the [printable checklist](pathway-checklist.md)
next to you while you film.

## Do these once (setup)

- Choose your platform(s): X, TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts. → [platform specs](platform-specs.md)
- Read the one rule: educational, not promotional. → [the rules in plain English](what-the-rules-mean.md)
- Bookmark the free tools. → [the free AI toolkit](toolkit-overview.md)

Then, for **every** video, walk these eight steps. (First time: ~90 min. After that: much faster.)

## Step 1 — Lock one idea · 5 min
- **Do:** choose one topic and the single thing a viewer should understand.
- **Open:** the **Idea generator** (menu → Create) and regenerate until one lands — or pick from [the six pillars](six-pillars.md).
- **Done when:** you can finish this sentence — *"After 60 seconds, the viewer will understand ____."*
- **Example:** *"…that money covers a little less each year because more of it is created over time."*
> ⚠ **Common slip:** cramming several ideas into one video. One idea per video — park the rest for next time.

## Step 2 — Fill the brief · 5 min
- **Do:** capture the plan so you don't lose it.
- **Open:** [the video brief template](video-brief-template.md) — topic, takeaway, audience, hook, platform.
- **Done when:** the brief's top section is filled. (See it filled in [the worked example](worked-example.md).)
> ⚠ **Common slip:** skipping the brief and drifting off-topic mid-script. Two minutes here saves ten later.

## Step 3 — Write the script · 15 min
- **Do:** write spoken words + on-screen text as a 4-column table: Time | Spoken | On-screen | Visual.
- **Open:** a [hook](hooks-library.md) for the first 3 seconds + a [story structure](story-structures.md) (Hook → Gap → Payoff → Loop). Paste the generator's **script prompt** into an AI chat for a first draft, then edit it.
- **Done when:** it's ~110–150 words, one idea, jargon defined, and it ends with "follow to learn more."
- **Watch for:** the [do/don't words](do-and-dont-language.md) — keep it about how the system works, never about what anyone earns.
> ⚠ **Common slip:** going too long, or letting a "you'll come out ahead" line creep in. Keep it ~60s and strictly about the mechanism.

## Step 4 — Get your voice · 10 min
- **Do:** record narration, or generate an AI voice.
- **Open:** [voiceover / TTS](voiceover-tts.md). Your real voice builds the most trust and needs no AI label; an AI voice must be labelled.
- **Done when:** you have a clean audio track of the script.
> ⚠ **Common slip:** a rushed, robotic read — or forgetting to label a realistic AI voice. Slow down; breathe between sentences.

## Step 5 — Get your visuals · 15 min
- **Do:** gather b-roll clips + a title card. Keep visuals abstract (a gold coin, an eroding stack of notes, a glowing network).
- **Open:** [video generation](video-generation.md) and [images & thumbnails](images-and-thumbnails.md). Use the generator's image/video prompts.
- **Done when:** you have 4–6 short clips/images + a title card, all vertical 9:16.
> ⚠ **Common slip:** realistic fake people, or fake charts implying a payoff. Keep every visual abstract and honest.

## Step 6 — Assemble + caption · 20 min
- **Do:** cut it together and add burned-in captions (most people watch on mute).
- **Open:** [captions & editing](captions-and-editing.md) — CapCut is free. A full free pipeline is in [end-to-end workflows](workflows.md).
- **Done when:** a rough cut plays start to finish with captions on.
> ⚠ **Common slip:** no captions, or a static frame for 60 seconds. Caption everything; change the visual every couple of seconds.

## Step 7 — Add disclosures · 5 min
- **Do:** put the labels on screen in the first 3–5 seconds and in your caption.
- **Open:** [disclosures explained](disclosures-explained.md). Always "Not financial advice"; add #Ad if you're recognized for posting; add an AI label if you used realistic AI media.
- **Done when:** all required labels are visible early and drafted in your caption.
> ⚠ **Common slip:** burying the disclosure at the end. It belongs in the first 3–5 seconds — on screen and at the front of the caption.

## Step 8 — Check, then post · 10 min
- **Do:** run the gut-check, then publish or schedule. Tailor the caption per platform.
- **Open:** [the pre-publish checklist](pre-publish-checklist.md) and [platform specs](platform-specs.md).
- **Done when:** it's live (or scheduled), with disclosures and captions in place.
> ⚠ **Common slip:** posting the identical caption everywhere. Tailor it per platform and lead with the disclosure.

## After: learn and loop

Notice what landed — the hook, the topic, the length — and feed it into your next idea.
To plan a whole run at once, use the [content calendar & series](content-calendar-and-series.md).

### The pathway at a glance
**Idea → Brief → Script → Voice → Visuals → Assemble → Disclose → Check → Post → Learn.**
One idea, ~60 seconds, every label in place. Repeat.
