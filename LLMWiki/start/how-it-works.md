# How it all works (start here)

Welcome. This hub helps you make short, **educational** videos about Digital Gold —
the kind people actually learn from, that stay safely within the rules. If it feels like
there's a lot here and you're not sure how it fits together, this page is your map.

## The big idea

You're not selling anything. You're **explaining how money works** and where Digital
Gold fits. Every video answers one simple question — like *"why does my money cover a
little less each year?"* — in 30–60 seconds, in plain language. That's the whole job.
(Why this framing? See [what the rules mean](what-the-rules-mean.md).)

## The journey, in five steps

Making one video looks like this:

1. **Pick one idea.** One video = one idea. Stuck? Open the **Idea generator** in the menu and hit Regenerate. → [using the generator](using-the-generator.md)
2. **Write the script.** A short spoken explainer. Grab attention in the first 3 seconds with a [hook that works](hooks-library.md), and keep it tight with a [story structure](story-structures.md).
3. **Make the visuals.** Film yourself, or go faceless with simple b-roll. The [free toolkit](toolkit-overview.md) lists $0 tools for every step.
4. **Add the disclosures.** Three little labels keep you safe. → [disclosures explained](disclosures-explained.md)
5. **Post and learn.** Put it out, see what lands, make the next one sharper. → [end-to-end workflows](workflows.md)

→ Want the hand-held version of all five? Read [make your first video](your-first-video.md).

## The one rule everything follows

If a viewer never gets a single coin, your video should **still** be useful and honest.
If it's only useful when someone "gets in," it's a pitch — rewrite it. Get that habit and
everything else falls into place. ([more on this](what-the-rules-mean.md))
