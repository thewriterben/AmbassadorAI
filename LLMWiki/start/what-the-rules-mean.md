# The rules, in plain English

The rules sound intimidating, but they boil down to one habit: **explain, don't sell.**
Here's what that means day to day.

## Why "educational, not promotional"?

Digital Gold is designed to be treated as a **digital commodity** — like a tool or a raw
material — not as something people acquire hoping to profit. The moment your content
promises someone will be financially better off, it works *against* that design. So we keep
everything educational. You're a teacher, not a salesperson.

## The one gut-check

Ask yourself: **"If a viewer never gets a single coin, is this still a useful, honest
video?"**

- **Yes** → you're good.
- **No** → it's a pitch. Reframe it to explain *how the system works* instead.

## The "rewrite reflex"

Whenever a sentence hints that the viewer will *gain*, flip it to describe the *mechanism*.
Same fact, safe framing:

- ❌ "Get in early — more coins for you."
- ✅ "Coins are released as people join and split among everyone, so each person's share gets smaller as the network grows."

The full cheat-sheet is [do / don't language](do-and-dont-language.md).

## The "safe harbor" trap (important)

You'll see the phrase **"safe harbor."** It's a **legal** term — it means DGD is designed
to qualify as a digital commodity, *not* a security. It does **not** mean "a safe place for
your money," and you must never use it that way. ([more](positioning-safe-harbor.md))

## Words to skip, words that are safe

- **Skip:** invest, returns, profit, gains, "buy now", moon, 100x, "get in early."
- **Safe:** money, sound money, purchasing power, scarcity, monetary design, mechanics, educational.

Full list: [do / don't language](do-and-dont-language.md).

That's really it. Teach how it works, add your disclosures, and you'll stay well inside the
lines.
