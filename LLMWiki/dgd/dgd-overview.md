# What Is Digital Gold (DGD)?

The subject you teach. A plain-language overview of Digital Gold for ambassadors, drawn from the White Paper.

> **Framing reminder:** Everything below is *how the system is designed.* Present it as design and ideas, never as "buy this, you'll profit." See [communications discipline](../compliance/communications-discipline.md).

## In one breath

Digital Gold (DGD) is a **Layer-1 cryptocurrency** designed to behave like **sound, wealth-preserving money** rather than a speculative bet. It was created by **John Wright Gotts**, founder of the **Digital Gold Foundation**, and developed from 2014–2026 (WP, title page). The founder funded development personally and donated the completed coin to the Foundation (WP Abstract).

A "Layer-1" is a base blockchain network (like Bitcoin or Ethereum) that runs on its own, rather than being built on top of another chain.

## The problem it responds to

Government-issued money loses value over time. The White Paper notes the Federal Reserve Note has lost **more than 96% of its purchasing power since 1913**, and that a 2%-per-year inflation target means a worker saving across a 40-year career sees roughly **half** of their lifetime savings eroded by debasement (WP Abstract). DGD is framed as a response to that problem, inspired by economist **Friedrich Hayek's** idea (*The Denationalisation of Money*, 1976) that private currencies should compete, and the best one for users wins.

## What makes DGD structurally different

1. **Built to be spent, not just held.** Most coins have a fluctuating bid/ask price, so a merchant who accepts them converts to dollars immediately. DGD uses a **single-price architecture** so a merchant can hold it and pay suppliers in it — letting it circulate through a whole supply chain (WP Abstract, §9). See [supply & distribution](supply-and-distribution.md).
2. **Fair, continuous distribution.** Instead of concentrating coins among early insiders, a **Proof-of-Participation (PoP)** model releases coins gradually as the network grows from 1,000 toward 80 million users (WP Abstract). See [supply & distribution](supply-and-distribution.md).
3. **A real valuation method.** The **Crypto Fair Value (CFV)** formula values coins against a fixed benchmark instead of pure speculation. See [valuation: CFV & DGSB](valuation-cfv-dgsb.md).
4. **Designed not to be a security.** Fixed rules set at inception that can't be changed, no staking rewards, fees burned, no ongoing company profiting from operation (WP Abstract, §12). See [compliance overview](../compliance/compliance-overview.md).

## The intellectual roots (great explainer material)

DGD draws on the **Austrian school of economics** — this is some of the most "teachable," non-promotional content you can make:

- **Carl Menger (1892):** money emerges naturally from trade, not government decree; the most *saleable* goods (durable, divisible, portable, scarce, widely wanted) become money — historically gold and silver.
- **Ludwig von Mises:** government monopoly over money leads to debasement and a wealth transfer from workers to the financial class.
- **Friedrich Hayek (1976):** strip the monopoly; let currencies compete.

These ideas synthesize into the **[Six Pillars of Perfect Money](six-pillars.md)** — the backbone of most educational DGD videos.

## The headline design numbers (state as design facts only)

| Feature | Design value (WP) |
|---|---|
| Total supply cap | 21,000,000 coins |
| Max circulating supply | 19,000,000 (2,000,000 held in treasury for staking) |
| Distribution driver | `N` = current number of users |
| Network growth target | 1,000 → 80,000,000 users |
| Consensus | Hybrid Proof-of-Work + Proof-of-Stake, refined by SegWit |
| Block time | 64 seconds |
| Transaction fees | Burned (destroyed), not collected |
| Privacy | Native Tor V3 onion address support |

> ⚠️ **Never** turn the price-curve numbers ($3.40 → $100,000 across the growth curve) into a profit pitch. They describe a *mechanism*, not a promise or prediction. This is the single most common compliance mistake — see [do & don't language](../compliance/do-and-dont-language.md).
