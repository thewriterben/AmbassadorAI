# Valuation: The CFV Formula & the DGSB Benchmark

How DGD frames "what a cryptocurrency is actually worth." Strong explainer material because it's analytical, not promotional.

> Teach this as a **measurement framework**, the way you'd explain how the meter or kilogram is defined. Do **not** use it to argue DGD is "undervalued" or will rise — that crosses into investment framing. See [do & don't language](../compliance/do-and-dont-language.md).

## The core analogy (use this on camera)

Every measurement system needs a fixed standard. The meter is defined by the speed of light; the kilogram by the Planck constant. They don't change just because the thing being measured changes (WP §3.1). Crypto never had such a standard — so Bitcoin's price became the only signal, and every other coin traded as a leveraged bet on Bitcoin (WP §1).

The Foundation proposes one: the **Digital Gold Standard Benchmark (DGSB)**.

## The DGSB — a frozen snapshot

In **December 2024**, Bitcoin first touched **$100,000**, at a market cap of about **$1.983 trillion** — valued not by a Reddit thread but by the world's largest asset managers and the regulated ETF infrastructure (WP §3.2). The DGSB freezes that moment as a fixed reference point. It does **not** track Bitcoin's price afterward; it's a snapshot (WP §3.3).

The benchmark captures four measurable fundamentals:

| Metric | Bitcoin @ Dec 2024 | Weight in CFV |
|---|---|---|
| **Adoption** (unique holders) | ~80 million | **70%** |
| **Annual Transactions** | ~6.09 billion | 10% |
| **Annual Transaction Value** | ~$13.49 trillion | 10% |
| **Active Developers** | ~905 contributors | 10% |

(WP §3.2, §4.) Adoption dominates because a currency's worth comes mostly from how many people actually use it.

## The CFV formula — plain version

**Crypto Fair Value (CFV)** measures any coin's fundamentals against the frozen DGSB and produces a "Fair Coin Price." If a coin's fundamentals improve relative to the benchmark, its Fair Coin Price rises; if they deteriorate, it falls. **Bitcoin itself is measured against the benchmark on equal terms — no special treatment** (WP §3.3).

This mirrors how gold worked historically: gold didn't change to flatter the currencies measured against it; currencies rose or fell on their own merits (WP §3.3).

**Why it's good content:** it reframes "is this coin overpriced?" from vibes into a transparent, reproducible calculation anyone can verify with public data (WP §1). That's a genuinely interesting, non-salesy story.

## Teaching angles

- **"Crypto has no ruler — here's why that's a problem."** (the missing-standard idea)
- **"Why every altcoin is basically a bet on Bitcoin."** (structural dependency, WP §1)
- **"How do you measure what a coin is *really* worth? Four numbers."** (the CFV metrics)
- **"They froze Bitcoin's best day into a permanent yardstick."** (the DGSB snapshot)

## Hard line for ambassadors

You may explain the formula and the metrics. You may **not**:
- claim DGD is "undervalued" or "will reach $X,"
- compare CFV outputs to argue someone should acquire DGD,
- imply the framework predicts profit.

Keep it at: *"This is the method the framework uses to assign a fair value."*
