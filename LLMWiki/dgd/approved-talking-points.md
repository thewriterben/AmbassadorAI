# Approved Talking Points (Compliance-Safe)

Copy-paste-ready statements that are **accurate** and **stay inside the communications discipline**. Use these as the spoken backbone of videos. Every line here is educational, not promotional.

> Rule of thumb: describe **ideas and design**. Never describe **profit, returns, or the future price**.

## Opening lines / hooks (safe)
- "Money you save loses value every year — and that's by design. Let me explain."
- "There are six things economists say 'perfect money' needs. Most currencies fail at least one."
- "Crypto has no agreed way to measure what a coin is actually worth. One project tried to build one."
- "Here's a test that tells you whether a 'crypto payment' is actually money."
- "The US dollar has lost over 96% of its purchasing power since 1913. Here's what that means for you."

## Core educational statements (safe)
- "Digital Gold is a Layer-1 cryptocurrency designed to behave like sound, wealth-preserving money instead of a speculative asset."
- "It's built on Austrian economic ideas — that money should be scarce, stable, freely chosen, and free to spend."
- "Its supply is capped at 21 million coins, with rules that were set at the start and can't be changed."
- "Instead of rewarding early insiders, coins are released gradually as the network grows, and split among all current users."
- "It values coins using a transparent formula anyone can check with public data, rather than pure speculation."
- "The design tries to let the coin circulate through an entire supply chain — not just get converted to dollars the moment a merchant receives it."
- "Transaction fees are destroyed rather than collected, so no company profits from running the network."

## Framing the price curve (safe vs. unsafe)
- ✅ "The price moves along a fixed curve set by the protocol as the number of users grows — it's a distribution mechanism, not a market prediction."
- ❌ "It's going from $3 to $100,000, so get in early." (price projection + solicitation — never)

## Honest, trust-building lines (safe and good for engagement)
- "I'm not a financial advisor and this isn't investment advice — I just find the monetary design interesting."
- "Do your own research and read the white paper yourself; I'll link it."
- "Reasonable people disagree about whether any of this will work. Here's the idea, judge for yourself."
- "A regulator could view this differently than the Foundation does — that's openly acknowledged in their own white paper." (WP §12.16)

## Required disclosures to weave in (when you have a material connection)
- Spoken, in the first few seconds: "Heads up — I take part in Digital Gold's ambassador program, so consider this a sponsored educational video."
- Plus on-screen text + a caption label. → [FTC disclosure](../compliance/ftc-disclosure.md)
- If media is AI-generated and realistic: label it. → [AI disclosure](../compliance/ai-disclosure.md)

## Positioning talking points (safe-harbor & anti-degen)
- "DGD is designed to qualify as a *digital commodity* — not a security — under the 2026 crypto framework." (legal claim, not financial) → [safe-harbor page](positioning-safe-harbor.md)
- "Its value is meant to come from the protocol and supply and demand — not from expecting a profit. That's literally the legal test."
- "DGD is the *anti-degen* coin — boring on purpose. There's nothing to gamble on by design."
- "'Safe harbor' is about staying outside securities law — not about being a safe investment."
- ❌ Never: "safe investment," "safe haven," "ape in," "next 100x," "regulators approved it."
