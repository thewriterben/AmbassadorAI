# Step 4 — Editing & Captions (Free)

Assemble clips, add animated captions, and export. **Most short-form is watched on mute — captions are non-negotiable.**

## CapCut quick-start (the 80% path)
1. New project → import voiceover + b-roll + images.
2. **Auto-captions:** Text → Auto captions → match language → generate. **Then proofread** (AI mishears terms like "Hayek," "DGSB").
3. Style captions: bold, high-contrast, 1–3 words highlighted at a time (karaoke style keeps eyes locked).
4. Add **disclosure overlays**: "SPONSORED · NOT FINANCIAL ADVICE" in first 3s; "AI voice/visuals" if applicable.
5. Beat-match cuts to the music; keep shots ≤ 2–3 seconds.
6. Export **1080×1920, 30fps**; check for any watermark before posting.

## Caption best practices (also an accessibility win)
- Keep captions in the **safe middle band** (not under the platform UI). → [platform specs](../craft/platform-specs.md)
- 1–3 words on screen at a time, synced to speech.
- High contrast (white text + dark stroke).
- Don't cover faces or key visuals.
- Proofread proper nouns and numbers — accuracy is your credibility.

## Editing rhythm for retention
- Cut on every sentence; never let a shot sit still.
- Add a visual change (zoom, new b-roll, text pop) at least every 2 seconds.
- Put your strongest visual **on the first frame** (it's your de-facto thumbnail on some feeds).
- End with a clean, non-salesy CTA card. → [communications discipline](../compliance/communications-discipline.md)
